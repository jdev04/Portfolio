Ways To Reduce

Manage Your Waste

Landfills and Waste Contributes over 17% of the U.S. Methane Emissions or around 114 million metric tons of carbon monoxide a year a way we could lower this would be to become a society that is overall less wasteful and to make sure we recycle so that more needless trash doesn't end up in landfills.

Electric Cars

Electric Cars are a great alternative option to gas powered cars. Electric Cars or Hybrids are a great way to save on rising gas prices and lower your personal commute emissions by 4-5% which may be small but is a huge step in the right direction.

Growing Plants

A great way you can reduce your carbon footprint and make air quality better is growing plants. Growing plants lowers Urban/Indoor Air pollution, Lowers Carbon Dioxide levels by 10-25% and reduces carbon monoxide levels by 90%.

Heating & A/C

A way you can reduce your carbon footprint is by simply lowering your heat and air conditioning before you leave the house. Air Conditioning uses 6% of electricity produced in the U.S. Alone and produces 117 million metric tons of carbon monoxide a year.

Efficient Windows

Energy Star Certified Windows can help you save not only on your energy and heating bills, but also help reduce carbon emissions. Single Pane Windows can save you $101-$583 worth of energy bills it also reduces 1,006-6,205 pounds of Carbon Dioxide which is the equivalent to 51-317 gallons of gasoline.

Solar Panels

Solar Panels are a vital part of the future of clean power, a study from Berkeley found that solar power can reduce home carbon emissions by up to 80%, this is great way to not only reduce your carbon footprint but also save on average around $1,400 a year. In 6-10 years they pay for themselves and are a great fallback for power failure.
